Select Features and Target Column¶

From the Project 3, we have 4 common features resulted from 3 methods which are Univariate, RFE, Feature Importance:

  • duration: last contact duration, in seconds (numeric)
  • euribor3m: euribor 3 month rate - daily indicator (numeric)
  • emp.var.rate: employment variation rate - quarterly indicator (numeric)
  • campaign: number of contacts performed during this campaign and for this client (numeric, includes last contact)

We're also interested in these features resulted independently from 3 methods of feature selections above:

  • age: (numeric)
  • cons.price.idx: consumer price index - monthly indicator (numeric)

The target variable:

  • y: has the client subscribed a term deposit? (binary: 'yes','no')

Observation

  • Based on the dataframe of feature importance of Decision Tree, we can see the "duration" is the most important feature which is placed at the top of the Decision Tree.
  • Following that, "emp.var.rate" and "euribor3m" are the next importance features placed at the 2nd top of the Decision Tree.

Decisions

  • The Decision Tree suggests that "duration" feature is the most importance feature affecting to whether a customer wants to subscribe a term deposit or not. Thus, a marketer can consider a marketing campaign focusing on customers having longer last contact duration.
  • "euribor3m" - an economic indicator, and "emp.var.rate" - employment variation rate are important features in the Decision Tree. It suggests that the macroeconomic situations (about economy and employment situation) affects significantly on customer decision to subscribe a term deposit. A marketer should focus closely on the economy changes and build a marketing campaign suitable with each economic situation to attract customers.
  • "cons.price.idx" - consumer price index - a feature measures the economic inflation which affects to the spending decision of customers. It suggests that a marketer should notice this CPI feature to make some adjustment on promotion campaign that can attract customers during a high CPI time period.
