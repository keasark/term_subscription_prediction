Decision for Feature Selection¶

  • Based on Univariate Plots, we choose "age" for numeric feature.
  • Based on Multivariate Plots, we can also choose "previous" for numeric feature.
  • Besides, we also want to discover "duration" feature which represents the last contact duration, in seconds (numeric), because the dataset author mentioned that this attribute highly affects the output target (e.g., if duration=0 then y='no').

Data Visualization¶

Bar Chart¶

In [16]:
# Visualizing data for age using bar chart
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

plt.title('Age distibution')
plt.xlabel('Age')
plt.ylabel('Frequency')
plt.bar(data['age'].value_counts().index,data['age'].value_counts().values)
plt.show()

Explanation

  • From the bar chart of "age" distribution, most of surveyed customers have age range from 25 to 60 years old. We can see that most of them are still in the labor age.
  • The mode is in the age range of 30 to 35 years old. This is the age that when people often have some years of working experiences and may leverage their career to senior or manager position.
  • This bar chart is also a positively skewed (or right-skewed) distribution where most values are sitting around the left tail while the right tail of is longer with some outliers.
